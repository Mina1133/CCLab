Puffins don’t live in houses or stay in one place. They wander, following the breeze, the stars, or just whatever makes their hearts flutter. But they’re never truly alone—because all puffins are family...

Appearance:

At its initial stage two worms with flower boddies find eachother and become one. As a toddler they have big cute eyes and a flower on top of their head. At the adult stage they have flower ears. From toodler unward they have small rounf boddies. Color varies between Puffins but always pastel colors.

What we know so far about Puffin? Before spring existed, the world was quiet and gray. The flowers were too shy to bloom, and the sun forgot how to smile. One day, two curious little worms met while chasing the same falling star. When they bumped into each other, they tumbled into a swirl of laughter and stardust. In that moment, they realized they were two halves of one whole.The puffin wiggled its way across the land, giggling and dancing, and wherever it stepped, flowers sprouted in surprise. Birds started to sing, breezes warmed, and the world lit up with color. Ever since, puffins have wandered the globe, following their hearts and waking spring wherever they go.
Puffins are born from unity, the beautiful result of two halves finally finding each other and becoming whole. They are led by their hearts, guided by emotion and instinct, which fills their journey with joy and meaning. As adults, they become symbols of hope and good fortune. Wherever they walk, flowers bloom in their footsteps, and they migrate across the world, bringing spring to every place they touch.
